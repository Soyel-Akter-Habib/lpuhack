Why Do You Want to;

to Code?

What really interests me in programming is the fact that you can impact so many people and it can start on your couch. This isn't why I started though.
I started because I had time during the day and because of a TED talk watched: And then the more you learn, the more you get into it and the more you enjoy the problem solving part. But I don't want to be a developer
I believe knowing how to program is one of the most useful assets nowadays for many different reasons that I won't get into here. But the big factor for me is the one I mentioned at the beginning and that I can explain with an example:

Last weekend, I went camping next to a lake. There were some paddle boats for rent there and of course they were a very popular attraction among tourists (we took one too). And a discussion that came up among the people I was with was the money they are making from these rentals. If you have 40 of them, one hour costs a little under 10$, you can make this much per hour, then per day, wow, huge amounts. Maybe that's the way it seems. The problem that I have with this business model (and the same goes with hotels, restaurants, etc), is that you can only make as much money as you have assets. If your business model is based around some sort of software (that can be started while you're sitting in your couch), the clients you have can use your platform 24/7 and the amount of potential clients is growing non stop due to an increasing population and internet being more and more available around the world. Pretty much unlimited market population (of course it depends on your product etc, let's not get into the details here). Furthermore, the new technologies that are coming are the type that blow my mind away when it come to this “impact” on the world all startups talk about. The concept of crowdsourcing, or crowd computing is revolutionary and this is what can lead to a more informed, more green world, not having to rely on big (and often greedy) corporations and their servers/algorithms. This is such a big change, such a revolution coming. But it's technical. VERY technical. I want to be able to understand it and be part of this change. You should to.
